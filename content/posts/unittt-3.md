## The Knight Capital Group: Case Study

While doing the case study I found many new terms and understand the usage of failure better. As the theme was professional and .… I feel the failure was worth studying, it gave a lot of perspectives and ideas regarding failure, 

The amount lost by the company is just 45 mins was astonishingly huge. This gives a practical idea of how minor bugs can pile up to cause such a massive failure. Many small mistakes in the algorithm collectively caused system failure and the company was pushed in the  position of bankruptcy. I can connect it to situations in life when  we could not reach to our desired outcomes due to minor mistakes

Reflections: While studying the case of Knight capital Group, I encountered two terms which I found worth researching more. The terms are **Reconciliation Testing** and **Feature Toggles.** In simple words, reconciliation testing is the procedure involving simple logics like testing two same systems paralelly for test the correctness of outcomes. On the other hand, the feature toggles is a procedure that allows you to enable or disable code remotely without the requirement of new deployment ( means it contains a switch which can be turned on or off without changing the whole system). It is used for automation.
Personally, I like the idea of feature toggles as it makes the task a lot more easier, and can be automated. 

Furthermore, I encountered lots jargons which made me think more about it and further research more on the case study. Learning about this failure was intersting. 


## Right Kind of Wrong: The Science of Failing Well

Understanding failure is a crucial part of the journey of learning as it gives direction to the path we're trying to walk onto .This books gives a whole new perspective to the idea of failure and exposes the human psychology tied to our brain. The author explained each term nicely, also I agree with authors' view regarding tackling the failure.I enjoyed reading the book. 

In this book, what was new to me was learning about _'Intelligent Failure'_. It basically suggests that even if we can't reach the desired goal, we'll learn something out of the process and eventually working on the failure will make us reach the final goal. So, making failures are crucial for the learning process.
